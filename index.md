# A

Below is a classification of a selection of famous philosophers into **Scientists** (those who primarily seek to describe, explain, or understand reality through observation, logic, or first principles—akin to theoretical science) versus **Engineers** (those who primarily design, prescribe, or build systems for human life, ethics, politics, or society—akin to applied engineering).

| **Scientist-Philosophers** (Descriptive / Explanatory) | **Rationale** |
|-------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| **Aristotle** | Developed systematic taxonomy of nature, logic, and metaphysics; aimed to *describe* the world (e.g., biology, physics, syllogistic logic). |
| **René Descartes** | Sought certain knowledge via method of doubt and deduction; foundational work in epistemology and metaphysics (e.g., "cogito ergo sum"). |
| **David Hume** | Empirical analysis of causation, induction, and human nature; skeptical inquiry into what *is* knowable. |
| **Immanuel Kant** | Critical examination of the limits of reason, space, time, and morality as structures of experience. |
| **Ludwig Wittgenstein** | Linguistic analysis to clarify how language maps (or fails to map) reality; later focus on use and meaning. |

| **Engineer-Philosophers** (Prescriptive / Constructive) | **Rationale** |
|-------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| **Plato** | Designed ideal state in *The Republic*; philosopher-kings, education systems, and forms as blueprints for society. |
| **Thomas Hobbes** | Engineered the social contract and Leviathan state to solve the "war of all against all." |
| **John Locke** | Built foundational liberal political architecture: natural rights, consent, property, limited government. |
| **Jean-Jacques Rousseau** | Proposed new social contract, general will, and educational system (*Émile*) to reshape human nature and society. |
| **Karl Marx** | Designed historical materialism and communist society as a solution to class exploitation; "philosophers have only interpreted the world... the point is to change it." |

### Notes:
- **Overlap exists**: Kant, for example, has "engineering" elements in his moral law (categorical imperative as a universal system), but his core project is explanatory.
- **Context matters**: Some (e.g., Nietzsche) could fit both—he *diagnoses* (scientist) modern decay but *prescribes* the Übermensch and revaluation (engineer). He’s omitted here for clarity.
- This is a **heuristic**, not rigid—philosophy often blends both modes.
